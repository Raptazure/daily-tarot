Seven of Swords

Barefoot and cowled, a man tiptoes into the night, clutching two swords. The rest defend him from whatever he flees- his furtive glance behind suggests an unwelcome and ignoble exit from some unhappy circumstance. Self-preservation has eclipsed all matters of honor and duty. Nobody’s hero, he is at least still alive to fight another day.

Sometimes it’s best to cut your losses. Words may remain unspoken, but the feelings that drive them will not be dispelled. Eventually your true thoughts will become clear, and you don’t necessarily need to be around when this takes place. This will isolate you, and you may spend many icy evenings in the wilderness, enduring a bleak existence. Ask yourself if cutting ties is worth this lonely lifestyle. You have a right to speak your mind. When you are forthcoming and strong you will never need to cloak your true feelings.
